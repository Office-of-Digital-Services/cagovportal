File insurance claims

If you have insurance, file a claim as soon as possible.

California protects you from losing your home insurance

by California Governor’s Office of Emergency Services (Cal OES)

State law protects residents by preventing insurance companies from canceling or not renewing home insurance polices for one year in areas near a wildfire. This 1-year moratorium applies to all residents in the affected area, including total and no loss.

Get help with your insurance claim

Employment

If you can’t work

by California Employment Development Department

You may not be able to work for many reasons. Here’s how to receive wage replacement during this time.

  • If you lost your job due to the fire, apply for unemployment
  • If you are sick or hurt due to the fires, apply for disability
  • If you need time off to care for your family and can’t work, apply for Paid Family Leave
    Apply if you need time off to care for family and can’t work.

Learn about Disaster Unemployment Assistance for the LA Fires.

If you’re looking for new work

by California Employment Development Department

We have resources to assist you in finding employment after the wildfire. You may qualify for job placement services, training programs, or other support through the Employment Development Department (EDD).

  • Apply for a new job on CalJOBS, California’s online job exchange system.
  • Jobs and training has general support, referrals, and training to find a new job in California.

Taxes

Income taxes

by Franchise Tax Board

People in Los Angeles County affected by the fires may qualify for a postponement to file, and pay taxes until October 15, 2025.

Property taxes

by the State of California and LA County

The state extended the property tax deadline for people in communities affected by the LA fires. You can apply for property tax reassessment with the Los Angeles County Assessor.
