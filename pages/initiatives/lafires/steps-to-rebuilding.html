File insurance claims

If you have insurance, file a claim now.

  • Make sure you document and track all recovery expenses.
  • Your insurance may also cover at least 4 months of rent for temporary housing.
  • If you need help with insurance claims, call 1-800-927-4357 or TTY: 711.

Get money to rebuild

You may be eligible for financial support to help you rebuild.

  • Apply for FEMA disaster assistance by March 10, 2025. Use this money to pay for living or rebuilding costs. If you have insurance, file a claim first.
  • Apply for a disaster loan from the Small Business Administration (SBA) by March 10, 2025. You don’t need to own a business to get this loan. You can use it to rebuild your home.
  • Contact your mortgage lender for mortgage relief. See this list of participating lenders.

Decide how to remove debris

  • Opt in to free debris removal from the government by March 31, 2025.
  • If you don’t opt in, you’ll need to pay for private debris removal from a contractor.
  • Learn more about debris removal.

Hire a contractor

  • Make sure you use a state-licensed contractor.
  • Get a written contract.

Apply for permits

  • You can submit your plans and apply for building permits now. You don’t need to wait until debris removal is complete.
  • You can also apply for permits in person at One-Stop Permit Centers. Make an appointment.
  • You can apply to put temporary housing on your property.
  • Learn more about permits.

Start construction

Your contractor can guide you through the construction process.
