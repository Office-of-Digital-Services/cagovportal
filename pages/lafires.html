2025 Early January Los Angeles County Fires*:
Resources for Impacted Individuals

If you have been directly affected, here are essential resources and information you can access immediately.

Major Disaster Declaration

On January 8, 2025, California received a major disaster declaration for the 2025 Early January Los Angeles County Fires, which supports the California wildfires and straight-line wind conditions in Los Angeles (LA) County. declaration made by the President of the United States.

The declaration allows FEMA and other federal agencies to provide aid, funding, and resources to support recovery efforts in the affected areas. Through this major disaster declaration, California is receiving individual assistance, public assistance, and Small Business Administration (SBA) assistance.

How Does An Individual Register For Assistance?

Federal Emergency Management Agency (FEMA):

Three ways to register for FEMA Assistance:

  1. Online at DisasterAssistance.gov. If you have access to the internet and your electronic devices have power, applying online is the easiest, fastest, and most convenient way to apply
  2. On the FEMA App for mobile devices
  3. Call the FEMA helpline at 1-800-621-3362: 4 a.m. to 10 p.m. (PST). Help is available in most languages.

To view an accessible video about how to apply, visit FEMA Accessible: Registering for Individual Assistance - YouTube

Helpful Tips and Resources for Insurance Claims

Resources to Help Recent Wildfire Victims: California Department of Insurance. Includes multi-lingual tips on preparing for an insurance claim before, during, and after an evacuation.

  • Ten Tips: TopTenTips_WildfireClaimants
  • Consumers should make sure any insurance agent or public adjuster offering their services has a valid license by checking online with the Department of Insurance
  • Download the Department’s Top 10 Tips for Wildfire Claimants (also available in Spanish, Mandarin, and Vietnamese), which includes information about claiming ALE benefits
  • Don’t forget copies of insurance policies, important papers, and a photo or video inventory of your possessions. An inventory can be completed quickly and easily on your smartphone and safely stored in a cloud

Disaster Recovery Centers

FEMA, the Small Business Administration (SBA), and local officials establish Disaster Recovery Centers (DRCs) to support survivors with centralized resources. These centers will help with federal aid applications, housing, rental support, and access to services from local, state, and non-profit organizations, with survivors able to revisit for ongoing help. Stay tuned for additional information.

Additional Information

Individual Assistance is available for individuals and households impacted by the recent fires in LA County. Individual assistance consists of multiple federal programs to help disaster survivors start to recover. These programs include, but are not limited to:

  • Advanced Financial Assistance
  • Housing
  • Rental Assistance
  • Funds for Repair / Replacement of Damaged Homes
  • Disaster Legal Services
  • Disaster Unemployment Assistance
  • Other Needs Assistance

Public Assistance provides financial reimbursement to state and local jurisdictions that have eligible impacts. Federal cost share has been approved to support emergency protective measures and debris removal within LA County.

Small Business Administration provides low interest loans for individuals and businesses impacted by a disaster event. SBA loans include:

  • Physical low interest loans for damaged properties
  • Economic low interest loans for working capital loans
  • Provisions to return evacuees to their home
