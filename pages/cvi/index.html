Submit a Certificate of Veterinary Inspection

We developed this page for veterinarians and officials to submit Certificates of Veterinary Inspection (CVI). They can easily comply with California’s requirements for CVIs for dogs and cats entering the state.

What you need to know

Who needs a CVI?

CVIs (health certificates) are required only for dogs being brought into California for sale or transfer of ownership.

Important: This requirement does not apply to cats or most privately owned dogs.

What does the law say?

According to California Health & Safety Code § 121720, anyone importing dogs into California for sale or transfer must:

  • Obtain a health certificate from a licensed and accredited veterinarian within 10 days before importation.
  • Send the certificate to the county health department where the dog will be sold or to the county where the new owner lives.

Where should CVIs be sent?

Use this form to send CVIs to local county health departments. This form is the official method to submit CVIs.

Do not send CVIs to CDFA or CDPH. These agencies do not process or forward CVIs to counties.

Need more information?

For more details, including rabies vaccination requirements for dog and cat importation, visit the CDPH Veterinary Public Health Section website.

Thank you for helping ensure the health and safety of California’s animals and residents!

When you submit this form, a pre-filled email will open in your default email program addressed to the correct local county health official. You will attach your CVI to the email.

Reminder: Be sure to attach your CVI to the email before sending.
