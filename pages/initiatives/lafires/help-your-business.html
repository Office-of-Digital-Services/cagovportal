Support for businesses

Small business support

by Small Business Administration (SBA)

SBA loan deadline: March 10, 2025

The SBA has many resources to help small businesses and entrepreneurs. This includes financing, training, and advocacy.

Emergency and disaster assistance for employers

by Employment Development Department (EDD)

EDD offers services to support employers impacted by disasters. This includes disaster relief payments, extensions for payments, and payroll reports.

Resources and steps for business recovery

by California Office of the Small Business Advocate (CalOSBA)

CalOSBA offers resources and support for businesses and workers impacted by the wildfires. This includes financial help, recovery programs, and workforce support.

LA Region Small Business Relief Fund

by City and County of Los Angeles

LA County businesses and nonprofits can get cash grants from $2,000 to $25,000. Submit your application by March 12.

Tax help and relief

Business income taxes

by Franchise Tax Board (FTB)

Businesses impacted by the wildfires may postpone filing and paying income taxes. The deadline for them is now October 15, 2025. Only Los Angeles County was granted postponement. If the principal place of business is outside of Los Angeles County, you must file and pay by the normal due dates.

Emergency sales tax or fee relief

by CA Department of Tax and Fee Administration (CDTFA)

Get emergency relief from penalties and interest. You can also get copies of tax records and up to a 3- month filing extension on your taxes.

Payroll taxes

by Employment Development Department (EDD)

Employers in Los Angeles and Ventura Counties directly affected by the Palisades Fire may request up to a two-month extension. Businesses may have extensions to:

  • File their state payroll reports: Reporting extension
  • Deposit payroll taxes without penalty or interest: Payroll tax extension

Property tax

by the State of California and LA County

The state extended the property tax deadline for businesses affected by the LA fires. You can apply for property tax reassessment with the Los Angeles County Assessor. Learn more about property tax reassessments and base year value transfers.
